Creating a formula to rank these 100 locations. Explaining why did I choose this ranking/formula, formula and the final rankings.¶

One of the soltion to this problem is creating a Ranked list of different Locations as per category; lets say a person wants to buy a car from a location having best administration or best overall experience then the person can just refer to the ranked list as per category and choose the location having best rating for administration or best overall experience.¶

Approach: 1) We can make use of 3 KPI's to derive a ranked list namely no of reviews, average rating and net sentiment¶

2) Adding the 3 KPI's to derive a Net Value column, we just cant assign a rank to the locations based on Net Value¶

3) As Net Value is just a distribution we need to know how the locations can be organized such that the distribution makes sense. For that we need to calculate z-score for every location such that it will give us an idea how locations are distributed in this distribution and how far are the locations from the mean of Net Value.¶

4) Formula for Z-Score = [Net value - Mean(Net Value)] / standard deviation(Net Value)¶

5) The Z-Score of the Mean of Net value is 0 which means any location having a higher Z-Score has a better Net Value which in turns mean that location will have a higher rank¶

6) Once the z-score is calculated, the rankings are assigned to the locations within the categories by arranging the z-score in descending order within the categories. The location with highest z-score in a category will have Rank #1 and so on and so forth¶

The above ranked list helps a person to refer to a ranking for a category at a given location and decide which location is abetter choice for buying a vehicle but this ranked list is not a good indication as this ranked list just gives a rank to the categories and not the locations but this can be used to choose a location by comparing rankings of categories.¶

Finding at least one location that is mis-ranked by the above algorithm (i.e. they should probably be higher or lower). Why are they mis-ranked? How to tackle this¶

The major problem with the above algorithm is that locations having bad average rating and net sentiment value has higher rank just becuase it has more no of reviews. This is logically not correct thus giving birth to the problem of over-rating and under-rating.¶

The location 4962_205 is mis-ranked by the above algorithm as it is evident that is has a mere average rating of 1.79 and net sentiment value of 0.5 but just becasue it has more no of reviews and a higher z-score it has been ranked higher.¶

Solution to the problem of Under-Rating and Over-Rating:¶

1) Instead of adding all the 3 KPI's we will add the 2 KPI's i.e. average rating and net sentiment to derive a Net Value column but we cant just assign a rank to the locations based on Net Value¶

2) As Net Value is just a distribution we need to know how the locations can be organised such that the distribution makes sense. For that we need to calculate z-score for every location such that it will give us an idea how locations are distributed in this distribution and how far are the locations from the mean of Net Value.¶

3) Formula for Z-Score = [Net value - Mean(Net Value)] / standard deviation(Net Value)¶

4) The Z-Score of the Mean of Net value is 0 which means any location having a higher Z-Score has a better Net Value which in turns mean that location will have a higher rank. But locations having same z-score will same rank, this challenge can be further solved by introducing no of reviews. If multiple locations have same z-score then higher the no of reviews higher will be rank of that location in the ranked list¶
